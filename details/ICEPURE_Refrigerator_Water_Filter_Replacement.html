ICEPURE Refrigerator Water Filter Replacement for Bosch Ultra Clarity Pro

Upgrade your Bosch Ultra Clarity Pro water filtration system with the ICEPURE RWF6100A replacement filter. Designed specifically for models like BORPLFTR50, BORPLFTR55, 12033030, 12028325, and more, this filter delivers clean, great-tasting water directly to your fridge.

Trusted by Standards

Backed by authoritative certifications, the ICEPURE RWF6100A filter meets high safety and performance benchmarks. It is certified under NSF/ANSI 42 by IAPMO, complies with European Food Grade Regulations EC-1935/2016, and is free from BPA. Additionally, it carries the Australian Water Mark, ensuring reliability and quality across multiple regions.

Superior Filtration Performance

Equipped with advanced filtration technology, this filter removes up to 99% of common contaminants like chlorine, taste, THMs, VOCs, particles, and more. It enhances the flavor of your drinking water, making every sip refreshing and pure. Meanwhile, it preserves essential minerals that are beneficial for your health.

Built for Longevity and Safety

Constructed from premium, food-grade materials, the ICEPURE RWF6100A filter is safe for daily use and built to last. The outer layer features a 0.5-micron filter, which captures even the smallest impurities. The carbon blocks are independently tested in a third-party laboratory for effectiveness against heavy metals and large sediments.

This filter is compatible with a wide range of Bosch refrigerator models including BORPLFTR50, BORPLFTR55, 12033030, 12028325, 12036454, 11032531, 11025825, 17005582, 17005585, WFC100MF, WFS200MF, WFS210MF, B36CT80SNS, B36CL80ENS, B36CT80SNB, B36CL81ENG, B36CD50SNB, B36BT935NS, B20CS30SNS, B36IT905NP, B30BB935SS, B30IB905SP, B18IF905SP, and more. Ensure your fridge continues to perform at its best with the right filter.

Simple and Hassle-Free Installation

Install the ICEPURE RWF6100A filter easily without the need for any special tools. For optimal performance, we recommend replacing it every 6 months or 300 gallons, whichever comes first. This frequency may vary based on your water quality and usage.
